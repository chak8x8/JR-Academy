3) Grid with gutters + 12 columns (common in design)

Template: 1fr repeat(12, minmax(0,60px)) 1fr → left/right flexible gutters + 12 content columns.

Why 12? It divides nicely: 12÷2=6, 12÷3=4, 12÷4=3, 12÷6=2 → very flexible for 1/2/3/4/6 items per row. The left/right 1fr “gutters” share leftover space equally (each gets half).

4) 14 columns — to compare

Template: 1fr repeat(14, minmax(0,60px)) 1fr. Here each card spans 7 → 2 per row.

14 works, but 12 usually feels nicer because of its many divisors.

5) Grid lines — “Start at column 2, span 12”

Think of vertical lines numbered left→right. You can say: start at line 2, take 12 columns.
grid-column: 1 / -1 (full width)
grid-column: 2 / span 12 (start at line 2, take 12)
grid-column: 1 / span 1 (just the first column)

Summary (plain English)

  • <main> = the main story. <section> = a chapter. <div> = plain box.
  • Using semantic tags doesn’t change visuals, but helps screen readers and SEO.
  • display: block (stack), inline-block (sit side-by-side), flex (1D rows/cols), grid (2D rows+cols).
  • grid-template-columns defines the columns. repeat(12, minmax(0,60px)) = 12 columns that can grow up to 60px.
  • 1fr gutters on the sides share free space. 2 gutters → each gets half.
  • Why 12? Easy math for 1/2/3/4/6 items per row. 14 is ok, just less handy.
  • grid-column: 2 / span 12 = start at vertical line #2 and take 12 columns (centers your content inside the outer frame).
